Overview

➡Rashtrapati Nilayam is the official winter residence of the President of India.

➡It is located in Bolarum, Secunderabad (Hyderabad).

➡The President usually stays here during the southern sojourn (winter visit).

📜 History

➡The building was constructed in 1860.

➡It originally served as the residence of the British Resident in Secunderabad during colonial rule.

➡After India's independence in 1947, it became one of the Presidential Retreats (along with places like Shimla and Ooty).

🌳 Campus & Architecture

➡Spread over about 90 acres.

➡Designed in a simple, colonial architectural style (not as grand as Rashtrapati Bhavan in Delhi).

➡The campus includes:

  • Main building
  • Beautiful gardens and lawns
  • Water bodies
  • Pathways and heritage structures

🌸 Gardens & Attractions

➡Known for its seasonal gardens, especially:

  • Mughal-style gardens
  • Herbal gardens
  • Rock gardens

➡Sometimes open to the public during specific times of the year (like Udyan Utsav).

🏢 Importance

➡It serves as a symbol of national presence in South India.

➡Important meetings, official events, and ceremonies are sometimes held here.

➡It is one of the three official retreats of the President of India.

📍 Interesting Facts

➡Unlike Rashtrapati Bhavan, this residence is more peaceful and less crowded.

➡It reflects a blend of history, nature, and governance.

➡The President's visit to Hyderabad is considered a significant event locally.
